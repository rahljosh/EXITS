Is ISE granted listing by the CSIET?
Yes. We proudly announce ISE has been granted a Full Listing in the CSIET Advisory List.
Does ISE promise graduation or a diploma?
No.
How much English can a school expect the student to speak?
Students must have enough English to be able to successfully participate in the ISE program. As can be expected, the speaking ability improves dramatically after a few weeks in the US.
Are the students expected to be treated differently?
Not really. Most students want to blend into the American school as quickly as possible.
What does a student expect to gain from his/her year in your school?
They hope to gain a greater understanding of America and to have had a cultural experience. In addition, they hope for the opportunity to take the courses they need for credit in their home country. Although graduation is never promised or required, most students need to follow a course of study which will be recognized in their own school. Failure to do so may result in that student forfeiting a year of credit at home.
Why would a student who has already graduated in his home country want to come to the US for a year?
Since ISE cannot guarantee a high school diploma, many students elect to complete their education at home before coming here. These students use their year in the US as a bridge to their future.
Will schools in other countries accept the US high school courses for credit?
It depends on the country and the courses taken in the US.
What is the role of the ISE Area Representative and the school?
The ISE Area Representative can assist the school with resolving any concerns that the school or student may have. The ISE Area Representative also secures permission for the student to attend that high school. The ISE Area Representative also selects and screens the host family and provides supervision to the student while he/she is in the ISE program.
Is the student subject to all school rules and regulations?
Yes!
