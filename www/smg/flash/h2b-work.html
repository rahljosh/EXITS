H2B VISA PROGRAM

What is the H2B Visa Program?
- Seasonal Work Program for non-professional non-US citizens
- Uses the H2B type of Visa
- Allows non-US citizens to work specifically for one company (employer)
- The length is a maximum of10 months, usually the average being 6-7 months
- The visa is not guaranteed through the company (employer), but it subject to Department of Labor, USCIS (Immigrations) and US Consulate approval

How can a company (employer) qualify for the H2B Visa Program?
- The type of work offered must be temporary. The program helps fulfill seasonal and/or peak staffing needs
- The positions offered are basic skills positions (this includes, but is not limited to: cashier, waiter staff, fast food crew member, ride operator, lift operator, etc)
- The foreign workers must receive a wage that reflects the average wage rate being paid to the US workers in the same industry and position

Benefits?

How the H2B Visa Program works?
- There are a few steps to be followed:
1. The work positions offered must temporary in nature.
2. The company (employer) must establish that there are no US citizens willing or able to work for those specific positions. This is established through a labor certification process filed with the Department of Labor:
    · Department of Labor gives the positions' advertising instructions (usually local newspaper ads for 3 consecutive days and 10 days recruiting period)
    · Department of Labor office sends results to the Regional Office and provides labor certification to the company
3. The workers are recruited overseas. For each candidate, there is an application filled in and a language interview to be passed
4. The I-129 petition (Petition for Non-Immigrant Worker) is filed by the attorney at USCIS (Immigrations) for the number of recruited foreign worker(s)
5. Once the I-129 petition is approved, the company is sent the Notice of Approval, Form I-797, and a hard copy of the notice is sent to the worker's country. However, this notice does NOT guarantee the visa issuance.
6. The visa interviews are held at the USA Consulate abroad. If the visa is granted, the applicants can come as soon as possible. If not, the candidates can be replaced.
