  E. D. Feehan Catholic High School
Mission
E. D. Feehan Catholic High School opened in 1967 to serve the needs of the Catholic community on the west side of Saskatoon. As a Catholic high school, its mission is to assist
parents in the development of students in spiritual, intellectual, physical, emotional, and social growth.
 
Features & Programs
   • As a designated French and Ukrainian bilingual high school,
      they are committed to providing students with the opportunity
      of becoming fluent in both official languages and an important
      heritage language.

In addition to these languages, classes in Cree and Spanish are also offered. E. D. Feehan offers a variety of courses for students in grade nine to twelve. These courses will allow students to meet
the graduation requirements of the Department of Education

   • The variety of programs permits students to follow a course of
      study consistent with their interests and abilities
   • The Advanced Placement program offers universitylevel courses
      and examinations for academically motivated high school
      students
   • The Honors Mathematics Program presents a variety of
      enriched and challenging opportunities for more capable
      students
   • The modified program at E. D. Feehan enables students to
      succeed in a high school program modified to meet the needs
      of students in relation to reading ability and particular skills
   • ESL
