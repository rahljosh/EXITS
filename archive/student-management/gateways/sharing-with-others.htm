Changing Our World...
Provided by Sheryl Davis, ASAI Area Representative – Northeast Region

As we look around us, we see a world in need of compassion and peaceful action.  As we look in our neighborhood, we see the people who are willing to engage in changing the world.  H.E.L.P. has inspired the Northeast Region student exchange families to design an ongoing program to meet some very specific needs:

  • Adopt-a-Soldier:  send cards, prepare packages, write letters to soldiers serving in the Middle East
  • Lend A Hand: prepare kits with blanket, nightlight, toothbrush, snack and small teddy bear for children in the foster care system or emergency disaster areas
  • Christmas Shoe Box: purchase gifts, candy, age-appropriate gifts and pack in shoebox along with a card for local children in need

Exchange students, host families, friends and neighbors participate in these programs throughout the year . . . these are ongoing programs that require many people working together for the good of others.

As you can imagine, good works like these require funding, so we have developed programs to raise the money necessary to fund our dream of making a difference.  We have hosted Spaghetti Suppers, prepared and sold Take-and-Bake pizzas, sold hand-made wreaths and even sponsored a Sock Hop.  It seems that the work we put into raising the money was almost as fun for us as it was for those who received special attention from us.

One of the wonderful benefits of our work has been partnering with other organizations throughout the region in order to achieve our goal of making a difference in the lives of others.  Each project has allowed us to demonstrate what a great program International Student Exchange is.  We have students from around the world creating relationships with American friends with the end result of a better world . . . one filled with compassion. 

We have helped dozens of children, college students and soldiers and, in the process, been examples of what can happen when we work together for the good of others.  The appreciation of those who received gifts from us and the students who helped are amazing..

from Marine Corporal Benjamin Wardell:

November 20, 2007
Dear Sheryl and Students,

I received your package a couple of days ago.  I must say “WOW!”  and of course thank you.  When I first saw the package lying on my rack, I didn’t know what to think.            

When I finally came to, I thought it was joke…Ha Ha.  And of course, once I saw my address I was overwhelmed with joy.  My Marines and I greatly appreciated the X-mas tree.  It now sits in the center of our room – decorations and all.  That tree brings a little bit of home to Iraq.

February 9, 2008
Sheryl, Students, and Host Families,

Hello everyone, today was a good day. Let me tell you guys why. Earlier, I was just sitting on my rack watching some movies and my buddy threw this package on my lap. Getting packages makes me happy, but when I saw who this package was from. I immediately became overwhelmed with joy. I can't tell you the level of appreciation that I have for such great support, even worldwide. haha. I know I said this last time, but it definitely means a lot to me when people take time out of their day to complete mine. So I thank you with all of my heart and am looking forward to seeing each and everyone of you at Bangor.
