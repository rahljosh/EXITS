Explore two of America’s greatest cities, New York and Boston.  Visit the diverse neighborhoods of Chinatown, Little Italy and Greenwich Village.  Marvel at the lights of Times Square and walk among the countless towering skyscrapers.  Then it’s off to Boston, the birthplace of American History.  Walk the historic Freedom Trail, visit Harvard University and Quincy Market.   Springtime in NY & Boston, Wow!

New York City – The world famous skyline with its towering buildings.

Visit The Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island. Stroll through Central Park. View the city from above the Empire State Building.

Explore great neighborhoods like Greenwich Village, Soho, Little Italy, and Chinatown. Walk along Wall Street and New York’s financial district.

Stop at Ground Zero. Shop on 5th Avenue. Spend an evening in Times Square. See a Live Broadway Show. Visit Rockefeller Center. See Trump Tower, NBC Studios, and MTV Studios. The Big Apple is truly a memorable place to visit: New York is The City That Never Sleeps!

BOSTON – Walking the FreedomTrail, a must see when visiting Boston.

You’ll make stops at historic sites and events that shaped American History.  See The Old State House balcony where the Declaration of Independence was first read to the public and the Old Granary Cemetery where many of the famous American patriots are buried.

Visit Harvard University, the oldest university in the United States.  Lunch at Quincy Market and go to the USS Constitution “Old Ironsides” and the Boston Common.  Come and see the beauty of Boston in the Springtime!
