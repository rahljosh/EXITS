Canadian High School Program

  Archbishop Oscar Romero High School
Mission
The school’s goal is one of meeting the varied needs of the students in the school community: academic, spiritual, social, and physical.
All courses will include a collaborative student-centered component to promote team building and problem- solving. As each student has gifts, the school’s goal is to foster those gifts to promote success and personal growth. All students feel that they are part of a community.

Features
• Archbishop Oscar Romero is a new school which opened for Grades 10 through 12 in September 2004
• A professional learning community school which emphasizes a collaborative approach to education among staff
   and students
• Emphasis on diversity in learning with a student-centered approach to learning
• Focus on community building and a personalized approach possible in a smaller high school with a strong faculty
   advisor program
Programs
Varied programs to serve the needs of students living in the community include athletics (a large gym and fitness centre), music, art, drama, broad CTS offerings (technology, food studies, woodworking, welding, automotives) as well as a strong academic program to challenge the gifted and to promote success for all students. The school has numerous clubs and activities to promote the development of life skills to encourage lifelong activities and interests.

Extracurricular Activities
Volleyball, Basketball, Soccer, Swim Team, Golf, Cross Country, Track & Field, Cycling Club, Student Leadership Team, Yearbook Club, Newspaper Club, Drama Club, Travel Club, Social Justice & Environment Club, Karaoke Club, Speech Craft Club, Fashion Club, Knitting Club, Salsa Dance Club, Super Cool International Languages & Culture Club, Web Design Club, Jazz Ensemble Club, and Cheer Team.
