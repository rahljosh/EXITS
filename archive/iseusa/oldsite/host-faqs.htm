How do I select a student?
Your local Area Representative will come to your home. After explaining the ISE program and screening your family, this person will provide you with a selection of students. One of the students will be just perfect for your family!!!
What does ISE look for in a host family?
Most of ISE's volunteer host families are typical middle class families. A family must have the emotional and financial stability to sustain a student for a year. They should have a desire to learn about other cultures and the willingness to share their life with a person from another culture.
Do families get paid to host?
No.
Can the student share a room?
Of course. The student may share a room with a same sex sibling within a reasonable similarity in age.
Does the student have insurance?
All students are covered by a comprehensive health and liability policy while an ISE student.
What does the student expect?
The student expects to be welcomed into a warm and loving home. Flexibility, a sense of humor and the ability to help a young person adapt to a new family and lifestyle are also important qualities that students hope their family will have.
If I have a problem, what do I have to do?
ISE provides support to all participants through its field of local Area Representatives, Regional Advisors and Regional Directors. ISE has a full-staffed corporate office that can assist all participants as well.
Who makes arrangements with the High School?
The local Area Representative will contact the local high school and make arrangements to have the student attend classes.
How does the student get to the host family's house?
The student's International Representative will make the flight arrangements. All attempts will be made to bring the student to the closest domestic airport.
Do the students speak English?
Yes. Students must have enough English to communicate and to participate in everyday life. From time to time, a student may appear to have poor English upon arrival. After a few days, there usually is a noted improvement in English levels.
